Introduction to Marketing & Image Creatives

Month 1, Week 1 Creatives Beginner

Welcome to Week 1 of the Marketing Intern Roadmap! This week you'll build your creative foundation — starting with an overview of digital marketing and diving straight into producing your first image creatives.

01 — What is Digital Marketing?

An overview of digital marketing channels, goals, and KPIs for e-commerce brands. You'll understand the landscape before creating any content — from organic social to paid ads, from awareness to conversion.

02 — Brand Identity & Visual Language

Understanding how colors, fonts, brand voice, and visual consistency communicate a brand's values. Every creative you produce needs to be on-brand, and this lesson teaches you how to achieve that.

Pro Tip

Study top-performing ads in your niche on Meta Ad Library before creating — inspiration from what already works is the fastest way to level up your creatives.

03 — Tools for Image Creation

Introduction to the tools you'll use throughout the program to create social media creatives:

  • Canva — Drag-and-drop design tool, perfect for quick social media graphics, banners, and story templates.
  • Adobe Express — Lightweight alternative with brand kit support and AI-powered design suggestions.
  • Photoshop basics — For when you need more control over layers, masking, and advanced image editing.

04 — Your First Image Creative

Time to put it all together. This week's hands-on assignment is to create three deliverables:

  1. A product post — Showcase a product with compelling copy and visual hierarchy.
  2. A promotional banner — Design a sale or offer banner optimized for feed placement.
  3. A story graphic — Create a vertical-format story creative with a clear CTA.

Key Takeaways

  • Digital marketing spans multiple channels — understand the ecosystem before diving into creatives.
  • Brand identity (colors, fonts, voice) must be consistent across every creative asset.
  • Use Meta Ad Library for inspiration — study what's already working in your niche.
  • Canva is your go-to tool — learn it well, and you can produce creatives fast.
  • Always start with a product post, banner, and story — these are the three core formats.

What's Next?

In Week 2, you'll move from still images to video creatives. You'll learn the hook-body-CTA structure, the 3-second scroll-stopping rule, and produce your first 15–30 second product video using CapCut and Canva Video. Always add captions — 85% of social video is watched without sound!
